ima747(Posted 2011) [#3]
Just an FYI, the blender scripts folder is a nightmare to find on mac, here's how:
2 things to know. It's INSIDE the app, and it's invisible too...
Here's a shortcut: in the finder, under the "Go" menu, select "Go to folder..."
Assuming you have the blender application inside a folder titled "Blender" in your applications folder on your boot drive, enter
/Applications/Blender/blender.app/Contents/MacOS/.blender
into the folder field. Adjust the path as needed to point to the blender app. That will open the .blender folder inside the application's contents bundle and allow you to drop in the script into the scripts folder. Be sure not to mess with anything else while you're doing this... there be dragons... Blender is a nightmare to get started with since it's designed for pro's first and no one else second, but it is an unbelievably great modeling suite if you can get through the crust, and all for free on any platform you like :0)
