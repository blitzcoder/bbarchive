Brucey(Posted 2014) [#2]
The main difference is that Blitz3D uses Directx 7, and MiniB3D uses OpenGL.

Another difference is that MiniB3D works on Mac and Linux :-)


Krischan(Posted 2014) [#3]
And some commands from Blitz3D are missing in miniB3D, Aligntovector for example. The new OpenB3D fork supports shaders and stencil shadows, we're still hunting bugs but it looks promising as a potent successor of vanilla miniB3D.
