RemiD(Posted May) [#6]
If you want examples for each pose composing an animation, you could try to capture your own motion from the front, back, side, that's what i have done to make these animations : rd-stuff.fr/animations-with-poseframes-emptyframes-500x375-10fps-HQ.gif

Basically i put a mark on the ground in my room which corresponds to the origin of the 3d world, then i capture the same motion, with 3 different views :
front view (i go from -Z to +Z)
back view (i go from +Z to -Z)
side view (i go from -X to +X or to +X to -X)

Then watch the video on your computer with a tool which can play forward/backward one frame at a time (the capture of a camera is usually 30fps) and try to see what are the "poseframes" and how many "emptyframes" there are between each "poseframe".
Then only create each "poseframe" in Fragmotion and let x "emptyframes" between each "poseframe", so that the speed of the motion is the same than the real motion.

Try it !
