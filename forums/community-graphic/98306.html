MadJack(Posted 2012) [#11]
Don't mean to hijack the thread, but I bought and played Hoodwinked last night and have a few observations.

Like others, wasn't totally sold on the heavily outlined art style - worked in suggesting a sense of grime but overwhelmed small objects, particularly when seen from far away.

Long load time after initial scene.

Also, after a strong start, became frustrated as it wasn't clear that on reaching the ground floor, I could walk right - it just looked like a deadend. This meant much wandering around while the hint told me to 'go to gound floor (sp) and click on girl'. This hint had me snarling as I didn't know who 'the girl' was. I expect others had this issue and quit.

Running/walk control was nerfed. It's important in an adventure game with a fair amount of wandering through screens, to give the player a speedy method of getting through previously explored areas. Expect this hurt review scores considerably. Also, many adventure games give the ability to highlight clickable areas.

Some action icons not working - possibly because trigger actions not yet done by player. Gives impression of bugginess.

Trouble sometimes in finding walk hotspots between foreground objects such as pillars etc. In fact, navigation often somewhat difficult.

Often playing in silence - needs more ambient sound fx such as footsteps, background city.

Not enough player feedback on what was intended in the collect the flowers mini-game. Given wonky nature of other action icons, wasn't initially sure if it wasn't a bug. Player 'trust in designer' issues here.

On returning to street level, double up of meshes for robot with chocs and street vendor - looks like new copy of animated model had been loaded without removing previous. May be related to alt-tabbing out of game temporarily.

Some character dialogue borderline racist?

For someone who's just lost his fiancee, main character a bit non-nonchalant?

What I do like; good voice acting, engaging characters, inventive situations, great music. Looking forwards to the next episode.
