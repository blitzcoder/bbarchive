jhocking(Posted 2009) [#2]
There are a few edges here and there that I would turn to even out the shading. For example, on the top polygon of the legs there's a really noticeable line in the shading because of the edge there; if you turn that edge so it's connecting the other vertices of that polygon the shading will look better.

Same thing on the feet. Just generally, you want to avoid having any really thin triangles like that. Instead of a really thin triangle and a huge one right next to it, turn the edge so that it becomes two more evenly sized triangles.
