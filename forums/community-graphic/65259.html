jfk EO-11110(Posted 2006) [#18]
the barrels definitely has too much polies. You should never become a finacial minister :o) Even new games use low poly barrels. There is a smart trick to get the impression of highpoly cylinders with a big lot of segments where they are in fact still low poly with 8 or 10 segments only: for the cap of the cylinder use a quad that overs the cylinder entirely, then texture it with a perfect round masked texture, a little bigger than the cylinders diameter, using dxt3 with alpha and the mask flag. Seen eg. in splinter cell for car wheels.

jfk EO-11110(Posted 2006) [#20]
I don't think it's slower. The cards are designed for dds masked things. As long as there are not too many overlapping alpha levels, it can be rendered fast. With masked dds textures, there is no alpha but the alpha channel will be used only to interpolate the mask contour.

For Shadow volumes you may use a system that allows to A use the orginal mesh for the volume creation, or B use a simplified hidden clone that fits the stencil shadow requirements.
