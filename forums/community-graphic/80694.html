chwaga(Posted 2008) [#26]
The models look heavily turbosmoothed...which is bad. Xylvan, if I were to be so rash as to give you advice, here it is:

When making humanoids, be VERY careful with their structure. It all too often helps to have a photographic or otherwise proportional reference handy. Unless you're doing a low-poly RTS model, NEVER box model organics. Save box modeling for props like cabinets and tables. Furthermore, a model should never have to be turbosmoothed to look good, in fact, turbosmoothing oftens makes models look even WORSE. Also, get your topology to fit your model. Bad topology too often ruins models. Watch your sharp edges (like those on the masks). Also, make sure your textures FLOW perfectly. Blurring isn't enough, there must be noise and color bleeding to make a skin texture flow. You should also use polygons and textures prudently, a necklace might be a good place to actually add polygons, because things like those flat look terrible. For humanoids, polygonal detail MUST go into the face, otherwise it will look terrible. As for UV's and texturing, be VERY CAREFUL on laying out your UV's. Avoid projecting from views, as that is a poor solution more times than not, and lead to ugly seams and incorrect distribution of pixels. Cut your seams where they are needed, and hide them well with textures. It often helps to apply a checkerboard texture on your unwrapped model to make sure that everything is distributed correctly (a common professional workflow method).

Hope that helps!
