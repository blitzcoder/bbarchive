sswift(Posted 2005) [#3]
Unfortunately, most companies that make useful programs/content still think it is in their best interest to severely limit what you can do with said content:

http://www.curiouslabs.com/article/articleview/866/1/429

You cannot distribute the actual mesh data for any models that come with Poser. Even if you modify it so much that it no longer bears any resemblance to the original.

I ran into this problem when looking for 3D models to use in games. Either the site licensing the model doesn't make it clear whether or not you cna distribute the model (Turbosquid) forcing to to assume you can't, or they specifically disallow distribution of the mesh data... even if you encrypt it with a tool like molebox... And even if the models are so low res that it's questionable if they're even useful for games at all at this point. (Geometricks)

There are very few sources for models which you can actually distribute with games. Generally you're gonna be stuck paying an artist to create them.

In short, Poser Artist is worthless for making games unless you intend to render sprites with it.
