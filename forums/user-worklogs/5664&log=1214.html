Stadium Editor(Posted 2004-06-26)
First up for me is the stadium editor. My goal is to create a dynamic stadium editor that would allow to create stadiums with preset models or by importing your own. The save sequence would save the list of models and coordinates for the models rather than save it to an actual file. Then the LoadStadium command would load those models before going into the game. At the end of the save the program will take a rendered screen shot of the stadium that will show up in all the stadium select screens, to show you what the stadium will look like.

Im going to start by adding some basics, and then trying to recreate all the major league stadiums. Once my feature set is full enough to recreate all the stadiums to some reasonable detail, Ill call it quits. Of course I cant send mlb stadiums with the game, but someone else can make them on their own. People make patches like that all the time. Game Concept(Posted 2004-06-23)
Im still looking for people who want to join the team, I could use another programmer and someone thats really good at 3d modeling. If anyone is interested email me at rob@....

I really believe that blitz3d has the capability to create quality sports games. Although it is impossible to meet the qualities of companies like EA who can do motion capture and 3d scanning, it is possible to compete with that market by offering a more affordable game, with better gameplay, and more customization.

Here are some of the features I plan to incorporate that will make this game different than anything else out there:

* Complete customization - The game will include a stadium editor, team editor, logo editor, and a league editor that would allow you to create any size or type league from your high school conference to a college league to the pros. Full customization means even though we dont have the MLB licenses, you can make patches on your own.

* Unique hitting system - Hitting factors will make this game the most challenging and unique ever. Hit placement and timing will be everything. Players will have unique swing speeds(I.E. Barry Bonds can swing in .8 of a second meaning he has longer to watch a pitch, where your no name player might have a 1.3 second swing, forcing you to decide ball or strike sooner). Players will be rated on a variety of hitting techniques from pulling the ball, to bunting, to sacrifice flies and more. Knowing your players means everything.

* Unique pitching system - control the pitch, location, speed, and spin you put on the ball. Throw killer curveballs at the cost of your pitcher getting tired sooner, or take a little off and hope it doesnt go out of the park!

* True player dynasty - Create yourself and start in a high school league and try to make your way to the pros. Infinite dynasties means infinite possibilites.

Just a few of my ideas.
