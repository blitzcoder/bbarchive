First Impressions(Posted 2008-05-13)
I'm creating a shooting game where the player is in charge of defending a beach from invasion. The player is able to walk around freely to fortified locations and use the appropriate defensive weapons to deal with the threat he deems most hazardous. These include, a bunker mounted anti-ship battery, an stationary anti-aircraft cannon, and an emplaced MG42. So far I've only completed the shore-mounted anti-ship cannon. The player at first will probably use this cannon to defend against landing craft and possible also sink some enemy ships. It is however highly uneffective against troops once they land on the beach. It is also useless against aircraft. The AA gun will not be very effective against troops either, but will most likely be used against aircraft. Finally, the MG42 is very effective against landing troops, but not as effective against aircraft and useless against ships.

How the game works:
Hundreds of landing craft will spawn (not at once, 15-20 at a time) and attempt a landing on the beach, while ships further out at sea, but still in range of the player, will be shelling the player. Also, aircraft will swarm in in sorties and do bombing runs and strafing of the coast in preparation for the landing troops.

The player will be required to achieve certain tasks to advance to the next scenario. For instance, sink 15 landing craft, or shoot down 3 aircraft, or sink a certain ship.
