MikeHart(Posted 2013) [#5]
From the help file:

Android : ...Sounds to be used ... must be less than 1MB in length. Longer sounds can be played using the music commands.

...not all audio formats are supported by all targets:

wav : glfw, android, ios, xna (sounds)

mp3 : flash, android, ios, xna (music)

m4a : flash, android (music), ios

wma : xna (music)

ogg : android

Finally, when designing for low performance hardware it's a good idea to try and minimize the number of channels used.





frank(Posted 2013) [#6]
You should make the sounds which you use with PlaySound as SMALL as you possible can make them in OGG format. If you have something big(ger), it'll work with PlayMusic, but you can only play 1 PlayMusic track at a time, so it's only good for background.

But all my issues disappeared when I just made the sounds really small (in kilobytes).
