poopla(Posted 2006) [#11]
Looks decent, but they are right about using a term like HDR for bloom. The more accurate description, AT BEST is tone mapping, in which you would render your bloom frame with the models having only grayscale luminosity textures(tonemaps) to weakly simulate HDR. HDR in full implementation is per pixel lighting to simulate the dynamic range of light we have in the real world.

(I can't remember the numbers, but in orders of magnitude of realism, without HDR implementation, rendering is at something along the lines of .0002 percent accurate :))
