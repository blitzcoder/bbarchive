Here you can see a magic effect in action. A little explenation of what is going on for those that are interested :
- The magic has just ended so the sphere you may have seen in other screenshots has already disappeared, but there are still some (green) particles that haven't disappeared.
- You can see that the magic hit two monsters by the hit damage that's displayed, and the impact effects (explosion-like particle effects). These impact effects show the spot where the impact was, but the monsters have already been hit back by the blow.
- The character that perfomed the magic has birds floating around his head because after attacking or using magic/items, he has to wait for a specific time (the birds get smaller and there is a time meter for this, that's not in this screenshot).
- The monster at the top has taken so much damage (previous hits in that battle) so he's dying, hence the transparant-outline-ish look.
- The lower monster has got a green-circle-effect around him, indicating that he's currently trying to perform one of his special skills (potion, first aid, fast forward, boost, etc).

Edit : the monster models are only temporary :)

fall_x(Posted 2005) [#8]
It's a system I tought of myself.
Skills are performed with "skill spheres", which are bought/collected. Better skills cost more, obviously. So any character can perform any skill, providing that you can afford buying them. This works pretty well.
Characters have level/exp, and when they level they gain points in their statistics. These points are divided slightly differently for each character, and you have some control over this. For instance, depending on which answers you choose in conversations, your character's "personality" changes (agressive, defensive, impulsive, open-minded, etc), and based on that, your character will level differently. There's other factors too.

What makes the game unique is the battle system. It's kind of a cross between a turn based RPG, an action RPG, and worms :) and personally I believe it is a lot of fun.
