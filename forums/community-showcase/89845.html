zambani(Posted 2010) [#6]
Thanks everyone for the feedback. Keep them coming. It's interesting that some find it easy while others find it hard. It might have to with the way gravitation works. The fact that you have to adjust the speed on one side but the effects are seen on the other side. Anyway, based on what I'm hearing from this and other forums , I might have to implement an "AI" assisted mode. But I'm afraid it might make the game too easy.
I original had the Orbits Remaining start of at 20 instead of 1000 but no one that tried it was able to pass round 1.
With only 20 orbits to start with, the ideas was if you completed a round in 5 orbits or less you would gain extra orbit on the next round. If you did it in more than 5, then you would lose some in the next round. When the orbits got to zero, then the planet blew up.

I was thinking maybe power-ups can be collected somehow and be used to do something cool to extend your orbits or give you more control or something. Any ideas.
I was also thinking that maybe on the round 20 or higher, you would only see the little white circles when you your planet goes over them, but most people aren't even making it to round 10.

Maybe add an asteroid belt somewhere. But how would that work? Just brainstorming out loud.

P.S. when changing your orbit, try not to speed up or slow down for more than a quarter orbit at a time. This way will make it easier to control the shape of your orbit.
