"Blitz" = "lightning" (fast)

That is the German translation of the word. It has entered the English vernacular from the Blitzkrieg style of German warfare in WWII and the Blitz bombings in London. It is used to compare the swiftness of your (product, procedure, idea, etc...) with how swiftly the German army would defeat its opponents. I personally do not find the term offensive despite its origins. WWII was a long time ago and the term is deeply rooted into our language. I just don't understand how someone can be so offended by the use of a historical event in a game, yet the blanket use of another historical event (in which 588 times more civilians were killed) is completely okay with him.

There is also a huge difference between an inaccurate portrayal of history such as Mainsworthy's game, and a complete twisting of historical events such as Hitler selling pizzas or legalized slavery in modern times. Also, trying to win an argument by comparing historical events to recent killings is completely in bad taste.
