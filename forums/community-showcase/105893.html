Derron(Posted 2016) [#5]
apostrophes are "truncators" or "left something out".

Exceptions are names. So "Harry's club" means "the club is owned by harry". When used with personal pronouns, you are using the genitive-s: "he's hungry" (he _i_s hungry). Other things like short forms ("I'd rather.." - I would/had rather) also use the apostrophe.

In your case ("the ships low on fuel") is using the plural form of "ship" 8so there is an "are" missing, and you are talking about multiple ships, each of them are low on fuel.


Dunno if this still matters: when learning English (somewhen in the last century) we got told to use the "long form" in written sentences - as a matter of style.

So in written dialogues we were tought to use "I would like to see" instead of "I'd like to see", "I cannot" instead of "I can't" ...

Maybe this would help you to overcome the issue with 's and is you wrote you have.
