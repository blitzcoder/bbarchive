Finally I have released a playable version of my coming game CTCC (Computer Touring Car Championship).

It's a 2D racing game and the demo comes with 6 circuits and 12 cars. All options are enabled apart from LAN/Internet multiplayer mode which is still under reconstruction.

You can play against up to 7 computer controlled cars and split screen mode is available for 2 players on the same computer.

There may be a fair number of bugs in the demo as well and I'm hoping that it will help me locate them and get them sorted.

Imphenzia(Posted 2005) [#17]
Glad to hear some of you like the game :) I'll try to provide some answers to questions here:

Licensing of cars will be impossible as I tried to contact manufacturers , they simply aren't interested. Therefore I will be forced to use dummy names in the proper release.

The timer is reset to 60 seconds every time a car finishes the race. I will change this so that the round ends when laped racers cross the finish line instead.

About acceleration.In proportion to real cars they accelerate at the same speed in real life. if you were to film a car from above, it will look slow as well... but i wanred to keep acceleration and top speeds authentic, although I had to keep away from real physics as it really doesn't work well in that scale.

Collisions: These were sooo difficult to sort out and to prevent cars from getting stuck and overlapped etc, i had to solve it this way. it is especially difficult in the LAN feature being developed.

Keys: You can change player keys in options menu. The reason arrows don't work in car maintenance menus is because there can be two players active at the same time, one of them may have arrows assigned. But to laptop users, simpply remap the keys in options.

Game play: I am aware and expected that a fair amount of players would be unhappy about the driving experience, but i developed to what i believe is a compromise between simulated acceleration and speed in relation to real cars and making them drivable on arcady 2D maps that look nothing like true tracks with their 90 degree turns etc :)

Driving blind: You can enable transparent bridges and tunnels in options menu to make it easier to see where you are going :)

I appreciate the feedback, so please let me know - thanks for comments so far.

oh, and full release will be over 100 mb - but high res circuits and music comes at a size cost, but it will also be available on cd... and i could release an ugly 1 circuit demo with no music for those suffering from low bandwidth.

Regards,
Stefan
