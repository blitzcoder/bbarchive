Well, I tried it and I have a lot to comment on.

I didn't run into any 'bugs' per say, but I definitely saw and experienced some things that I think could use some work.


The so-called bugs:

-First, the closest thing I experienced to a bug, was that the music kept playing after the game supposedly ended. Of course, it brought up your web site after the game ended, so maybe that was your intention to let the music play while the player browsed your web site??

-And the rankings screen had some serious slow down. Are you using 2D text over 3D?

-I once got to an area of MeltWater where I flew over a hill and basically fell out of the world.

-There are some graphical glitches in the rock formations in the latter parts of Sea Cave.

-The player placing (Pos. 1/6) is still updated even after the finish line. It should show the placing that you earned, not the current placing.


The goods and the bads:

-Level designs that I saw so far were actually quite interesting and worked well for the game. The shortcuts fit in to the game well, and I liked that you could pretty much boat anywhere you wanted to (even if it was a bad idea).

-The oasis level background needs something to spice it up. It was rather blocky.

-I did find the end of the MeltWater level extremely frustrating (although cool looking). Trying to race through those floating bergs at high speed was damn near impossible. ESPECIALLY considering that the collision radius for the boats felt way too large and caused more bangups than should be happening (can't fit through tight areas).

-I really liked the water reflections. I ended up playing the game with them on and turning off other features instead to get a decent framerate.

-You have some cool boat designs.

-The abilities of the boats were definitely noticeable while playing the game.

-The boosts are very short lived. In my personal opinion, I think they should last at least twice as long.

-The abilities of some of the boats don't match the boat types. For instance, a boat with a dish shaped bottom should slide better than one with a split hull. Also, I would expect a split hull boat to corner better. But worst of all, the hovercraft definitely should not be the fastest boat (are you serious?)

-Most sounds in the game are OK.

-The "Time Extension" phrase gets old really quick.

-I really digged the music.

-The Countdown (3,2,1,Go!) and Time Extension is hard to see on some levels.

Gabriel(Posted 2004) [#21]
'waste of internet bandwidth', if i use a compressed image format will it make a difference to the filesize even though its zipped (i know f-all about compression)


Not if you leave them as 24 bit color BMP's, but you can have 256 color PNG's ( which in many cases may be unrecognizable from their 24 bit color versions ) and those will be considerably smaller, even when packed.


oh and the soundformat is mp3, never heard of ogg, what is it, is it better and can i convert?


Ogg is better, if only because you have to pay a hefty license fee to use MP3 in a game. The quality is very similar and the filesize is *possibly* a little lower with Ogg. No bigger though. Yes, you can easily convert with DBPowerAmp. It even has shell integration so you can just right click on an MP3 and there's a "Convert to Ogg" option.
