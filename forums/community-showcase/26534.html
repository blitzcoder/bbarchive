Al Mackey(Posted 2003) [#1]
I've been working on my Ragdoll physics engine again (and ignoring my almost-complete shooter). I've been determined to find something useful to do with it that doesn't involve dragging around dead or unconsious bodies.

On a whim I tried using it to control the limbs, cloth, and such on a walking model, and make them dangle or be moved in a way that looks natural. The results are much better than I had hoped! The code was put together inside of a day, so it's a little rough around the edges, but it shows great promise. As you play, keep in mind that there are NO pre-made motions applied to the model.

Download here: Walk.zip



On a side note, I texured the Jaguar model using Lee Page's Tattoo.

Al Mackey(Posted 2003) [#16]
I don't think they're technically Verlets, but they're sort of similar. The main difference is that my BodyPoints track what angle they're at, which allows them to easily restrain angles. Another difference is that they have a simple hierarchical parent-child relationship with each other, instead of a cage made of springs like Verlets would have.
