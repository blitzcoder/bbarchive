mmmm.... thinks

ok, you have your senators face on and your board top down. really (if i'm being pedantic) they should be on or the other to make them sit well together.

You are also using very block color to denote different groups of seniors. Of which there is one graphic per group.

OK, using the card method of construction (we looked at previously) where a card is boult from a small set of primitives, each being coloured. we can use the same here:

We take a graphic of a senator (a nice one not a comedy one, and not a photo). we first separate it from the background - using photoshop or whatever, but this can take time to master well.

now for the groups. these are denoted by the color of the toga. so we need to cut this out and make it grey (we will add the color later)
And while we are at it, lets do the same for his shirt and hair. that was we can have many, many different senators with just these.

Now, I have already done this for you: width is 106

You can see the full senator first - draw that with SetColor 255,255,255

next up is the shirt, for each senator, come up with a color and draw it on top

after that is his hair. setcolor 40,40,40 would be sorta black, setcolor 0,250,0 would be green although he might not back your proposal with green hair!

Lastly is the base and toga color - set this to setcolor 250,150,150 for red. You can play with the colours, but again keeping them sorta in range of each other is a better bet, but it gives you something to begin with and a great place to experiment :)

lastly. Purple was expensive and usually only worn by the emperor, etc. lower grades would probably have more muted earth tone shirts? ochres, buff, browns, tan, etc

ok. that the senators sorted. I wracked my brains about the next step for a bit and came up with this:
we are now looking head on, so can use some artistic licence and drop half the senate and concentrate on just the facing bit.

There are three tiers so we will need 3 steps and a background:


I've used bucket loads of artistic licence here, if you are wondering where the bg comes from it's actually a 3d render that is then modded and re photoshopped with the steps, etc

So a senator should now sit just in the middle of each step:


You can see how I've desaturated the bg and blurred the far bg to make the senator pop out, using different coloured senators, you now have a senate to play with.

One last thing
The 3 tiers of the senate also denoted their rank. high ranking at the front, low ranking at the back

using a bit of thought in a group there should be 1 high rank, and 2 low ranks, middle rank could be 1 or 2?

if you (the player) started as a tribune (lowest consul rank representing the people) the aim could be to work (bribe, assassinate, etc) your way up the ladder to become emperor

That would give you a name: Beat The Senate

ok one (really) last thing. The text. if we are going all roman, then it should be a sort of bold times (one with those pointy bits)
