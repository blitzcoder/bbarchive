You can't copyright a word, but you CAN trademark it... which Taito actually did:

https://www.trademarkia.com/colony-7-73457708.html

...But you're in luck, since apparently their trademark was canceled / expired in 2005, so you're probably OK using the phrase now.

Derron(Posted 2013) [#10]
You do not have to care about trademarks if they are not registered in your country. There is no "global law" concerning that area (some people whish it would exist... but I am getting sick when thinking on the Americanization of our German immaterial-laws aka patents/trademarks...).

But - if you want others to make it available (download sites, publishing, ...) in a country where the title is trademarked these persons will run into problems (was forced to pay a bigger amount of money for providing a freeware game years ago - in the Netherlands the background image did not break a right but in Germany the rising Sun on red background is a registered trademark/picture-mark (dunno the word) for digital and analog games).

So it may not hit YOU but others to use a registered word/picture/soundstyle/dna/...
