GfK(Posted 2014) [#47]
Out of curiosity...

how long do you think it takes you to develop a game like this from initial concept to first sale?
I think this was about 10-12 months development, then probably another two months between submission and release. So, concept to first sale, say, 14 months?

How much of that time is spent on the "polishing" stage?
I tend to polish stuff as I go, but the final buff-up would probably have been 4-6 weeks.

How do you cope between games, ie during development when I imagine there is less cash coming in...do you just live off the proceeds of the previous game?
That's the idea. The revenue from game 1 has to get you through to game 2, and so on. I get paid minimum wage of £6.50/hour and we get by. I could pay myself double that but NOT taking as much as I can is part of a contingency plan because you never know how far the money your business has got is going to have to stretch.

It certainly doesn't seem the most hmm stable or secure of working / living arrangements. Not that that is necessarily a bad thing...I mean there are perks like setting your own hours and not answering to anyone.
It's not, but since my last "proper" job made me suicidal (and I'm not even exaggerating), it's the lesser of two evils.

I've heard you say (or at least I think I have) on these boards in the past that you could never go back to a 9-5 working for a boss type job.
Yep, but right now the insecurity is testing me. Crime Solitaire 2 did OK, but not nearly as well as I'd hoped for so it's definitely caused me a confidence crisis that means I haven't completely ruled out the option of just rolling over and giving up.

Oh, and I want a Bentley. :)
