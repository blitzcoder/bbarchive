A publisher wasn't too hard to find, I was supprised. Josiah (Chronic Logic) kind of found me. I just showed one of the games I made at the IGC last year and the day it was over I got an email from him. I showed him the prototype of Kingdom Elemental and the rest, as cliches go, was history ;)

I'd say the most important piece of getting a bonified publisher is having a *complete* prototype. The most import thing is to show you can actually finish something (also doesn't hurt to have other finished games, even if they are 'just for fun' games). So have a fully playable demo, it doesn't have to be insane, just like 2 guns or units, and 1 bad guy. But if it has a definitive start and end, they will see you are serious and can finish something you set out to do.
