jfk EO-11110(Posted 2006) [#9]
Finally I have found some time to listen to your music.

Great work, here are some suggestions.

ConflictOmegaOverture.mp3 has a good potential for an intro. The syntetic sound, especially of the strings should be avoided if possible. Maybe you can replace the soundbanks, or add some fx that makes the synthesis less obvious.

StudioComp.mp3 are two snippets of studio recodrings, as far as I see. The female singer of the first part is AAA. Reminds me a lot of shinead o connor. The seconds part, speed/death metal isn't exactly what I prefer, tho sounds well done.

TheAftermath.mp3 is extremaly sad and slow. Not sure if I could live with it for a longer time :) For games design it may be useful in a Game-Over cutscene, or something like that. The synthesis is less disturbing than in Conflict Omega.

WelcomeHome.mp3. Again the synth strings "bite" kind of. It is also pretty sad, kind of "tried, failed, who cares" theme.

It is of course a great classical composition. Strong emotion that reminds of composers like Wagner or Brahms. (Although your themes lack of some certain kind of harmonic originality, but this doesn't have to be a problem)

There's a lot of potential. I'd suggest get better strings. For games you should try to use emotions that fit the gameplay: tension, fear, horror, honor, speed, epic etc.

Well that's just my 2 cents.
