Xylvan, if you happen to be 12 years old, I'd like to congratulate you on your first experiments and foresee great things for you in the future.

If you're over 50 years old, I salute your courage and stamina to dabble in the world of computer games, which appears to be so dramatically far our of your comfort zone, but you're dong a great job.

If you're anywhere in between those ages...but have a diagnosed condition of some sort, forgive me for coming on so strong. If you're generally in good health and have no other pathological damages, I'd really suggest to have some higher aspirations, consult with a decently experienced programmer and designer and make smaller steps to improve the fundamentals of your "engine"!
Don't just run with a dreadful execution, trying to stomp some ideas into them and then fish for patronization here! It's not good for you, really, it isn't.

Here is what I would sincerely suggest to you:

1. Scrap everything!

2. Make a little movement "sandbox", where you try to have the dog move one the ground as if the paws would stick to it. Try to make it as realistic as possible when it turns and walks about. It's a matter of taste, but I think the dog could be cute, if it was at least moving someone decently. Consider gravity, level (balance) and so on...consider inverse kinematic for the legs!

3. figure out nice camera accompaniment, how to control camera behavior and blend camera animation based on queues, for examples.

4. work on shading. Try to figure out how to setup nice lighting, cast proper shadows and so on.

5. general look. Figure out shading methods for post processing to make it visually more compelling with little effort, for example.

6. interface design. If you can't have any elegant ideas, try to look at other interfaces and let them inspire you. Consider key elements of your main character, or where it would live, to embellish or even just design the interface.

Once you have a decent step 2 up and running, worthy of sharing, you could then make a post to present it, for example. That could potentially be interesting for other people to see and you can expect more direct feedback. Attach a download, so people can try walking around with the dog, no environment, just a bumpy ground plane, possibly with humps or even hills and valleys to walk through, just so show that it acts properly in more complex scenarios.

Upshot, if something is so incredibly simplistic, the few things that are there should really shine. Thus far this all looks like some silly sketch to demonstrate some weird idea for a very, very tight circle of close friends and not really the general public.

I don't know any of the background about you personally or the game or any of this. And that really does not matter either.
I think a person with your uncanny stamina has fantastic potential to pull of crazy games, especially if you had some help. Be it to take over certain parts of the production or simply to support your own growth as a programmer and designer. You should hold your head up high, keep enjoying to do all this, because you must have great pleasure in doing it, but strive for greatness and not feedback or what ever the reasons may be for you sharing all this so prominently.
