JustLuke(Posted 2009) [#14]
Looks promising. Some vibrant, dramatic terrain lighting would work wonders though. As it is, you have very bright, shiny weapons which only serve to highlight the "drabness" of the environment. Expanding a little more on this clash of styles, I'd actually rethink the appearance of the terrain all together, if I were you. You seem to have adopted a "realistic" environmental style, which doesn't mix at all well with the more cartoony weapons and monster models. Don't be afraid of contrast and vibrant colours! Dark shadows, bold colours - the careful use these would really improve the visual fidelity.

I'd give the sky a more vibrant colour too and add more interesting looking, swirly clouds. Maybe stick a couple of suns up there, too. Litter a places of interest around the environment; some odd rock formations here, some weird clumps of vegetation there, perhaps a broken down bridge that leads to nowhere; stuff that not only looks quirky and interesting, but that can be used to dodge around, hide in, and climb upon.
