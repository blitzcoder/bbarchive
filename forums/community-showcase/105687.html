M-ASL is a WW2 infantry game with MGs,Leaders,SupportWeapons(Granaides) Phased based like ASL sort of thing, its free 2D hex&chit, more complex because of HMGs, you get to control large stacks, giving targets in a stack for any shot, this allows you to fire at what you want and from where you want , one stack could fire at 8 different targets, thi UI is more hard because of the extra detail to move in 8 different wats or fire in 8 different ways per stack. the phases are turn-based

Ive included a Hotseat version aswell allowing you to play both sides with enforced rules, and its great fun

The game has evolved into a really good and deep strategy game, Melee a stack to shake units before they can attack you, or create large stacks to fend off melee but large stacks are bad against ranged fire, push your leaders to help in attacks use grenades and MGs to add the firepower.

the game is free

On a tablet you often do not have "shift buttons".

In my game (TVTower) I implemented "long press" to simulate right clicks.

you need your own "MouseHit()/MouseDown().." evaluator then (a "click" is if a mouse was down and is now up... if it was down for longer than XXX milliseconds, it was a "long press" and could get evaluated as "right click")
