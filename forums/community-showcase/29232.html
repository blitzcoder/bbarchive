FreeWorld Online (a MMORPG being made in BlitzBasic3D) is now accepting signups into testing of the game.
Signups will be able to participate in the next phase of testing starting on the 17th of January.

The team has worked hard over the last phase to add numerous new features and many additions to the already extensive game content.
We've seen the addition of:
+More character adjustment such as height and weight
+Dual handed and double handed weapons
+Food, drink and alcohol
+Camping gear for adventurers on the move
+Levelling pets
+Safe trading between players
+New in-game help system
+Almost doubled the number of items to over 500 (weapons, armour, monster drops, etc)
+Almost 20 new monsters/NPCs and improvements to existing one
+New quests
+Music re-mastered and added to


Preview of the new race being added for the start of the phase


If you want to read more about the game visit the official site at www.freeworldonline.com
To read more about what was added last phase; read the review of phase 6
More screenshots can be viewed at www.freeworldonline.com/sshots.php

You can signup to test the game for the 17th at www.freeworldonline.com/signup.php (no signups will be accepted after the 15th of January, first come, first served basis)


Thanks for reading!
The FreeWorld Team

King Dave(Posted 2004) [#18]
Wow, ty Tranz... your answers are almost all serious :D

hehe, additional/real answers (:P tz):

"how many persons are working on that ? Is it full time ? Professionnal team ?"
Roughly 15 people are on the team, although we have some general helpers for game modertation/public relation work. Some members work full time, but the majority do it in their spare time... no one is getting paid for this (yet).

"is FreeWorld made really in Blitz ? Only in Blitz ?"
Currently, yes. The server is likely to be re-written in C++ due to some flaws in blitz native TCP/IP support that will cause problems in the long run.

"if yes, then do you think that Blitz was well adapted ? Or C++ would be better?"
Peformance wise, I believe C++ would have been a better choice. But time wise and overal ease of creating in Blitz was definetly the best choice and I'm glad we're using it.

"did you use BlitzPlay or your own library for the network ?"
As tranz said, it is all original

Yes, but if we do buy and play... then eventually sales will slow down, and then we won't be able to afford to keep the servers up for the players.
