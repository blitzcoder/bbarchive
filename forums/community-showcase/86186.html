JustLuke(Posted 2009) [#28]
Critique based purely on the screenshots:

The dog sprites look nice when static. I've not played the demo, so I've no idea how well they're animated.

The mix of pre-rendered 3d and traditional 2d art doesn't work. It all looks a bit jarring and mismatched. Not only this, the 3d elements are poor quality, which is a shame when they're coupled with nice looking 2d sprites.

The grass texture needs work. It's too busy and it repeats too much - it needs to be simplified and mixed with some other little details for added variety.

As a whole, the screenshots are too washed out. Tweak the contrast.

Take a look at some 16 bit era platformer games for clues as to how you can improve the overall look of the game. The SNES Mario games are particularly great examples. Note the clean lines, bold colours, and simple textures.

Pay more attention to the interface - slick, simple icons, a nice anti-aliased font, and careful screen placement would improve things no end. Remember, the sprites move about and the background scrolls, but since the player must stare at the same interface elements 100% of the time, they need to be pleasant.
