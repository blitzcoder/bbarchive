@GfK Yep all my own IP so it shouldn't get taken off sale. Fairway did at least get 5 years before they swapped it out for their version.

@Why0Why Glad to hear you both enjoyed it! Thanks! :-)

I am curious though, to what extend sales of these games are seasonal -- I'd expect it would do fairly well during autumn, but probably plummet once Christmas hits.

Any plans to do another non-seasonal 'neutral' game, like your wizard of Oz?

Well after Spring Bonus I said to myself that I'd never make seasonal games any more so that I could have games that sell all year round, and in fact I have several like that planned. But then this summer I basically ran out of money and thought I could make a "quick" seasonal match-3 that would help cash flow. However, once I planned it out I ended up paying a coder to make quite a lot of additions to the engine and paying quite a lot for the art. I'm glad I did that though as it has really paid off. My next game won't be seasonal though.

You are right that there's a big drop off in sales after the seasonal event. For example on BFG Holiday Bonus sales drop to as low as 2.5% of December sales during March but by Aug they begin to pick up again until they are quite big in November (it's the second biggest month). Also sales in January aren't too bad. I also ask all the portals to repromote the game every year and many of them do that. You can't get a non-seasonal game repromoted as easily but it is possible. So seasonal games have a long thin very spiky tail, and non-seasonal games have a long thicker tail. Make sense?

@therevills There appears to be, yes! In fact a lot of players say they are fed up of gloomy HOGs and like the chance of vibe/pace my game gave them. I was of the opinion that unless you spent a LOT of time/money on making a top quality match-3 that you could only make a certain OKish amount of money and that card games had a better chance of making good money (based on your games, Roman's and GfKs) but it seems that it is possible to construct a fairly low-budget match-3 that does great! That's an eye opener for me anyway. I do believe I got a lot right with this game though based on my experience and that probably helped a lot - I have made a shitload of match-3s now after all ;-)

As much as I personally despise the game, it's probably the likes of Candy Crush Saga that's breathed new life into an old genre.
Maybe a bit with people who'd never played match-3s, but to be honest I think that original fans of the genre didn't really go away it's just that HOGs were outselling them and devs are making less match-3s these days. All it took, for me, was a decent quality one with the right theme at the right time to do well.

@Remi thanks. Yep it is indeed motivating for me! That's my 9th commercial game (excluding mobile ports) and I'm working on my 10th now.

I took at look at Spooky Range. Looks kinda fun but I guess people who are into action games will just buy the big budget ones.

Grey Alien(Posted 2015) [#60]
Yeah the humble store is the better deal. Also devs get 5% more and charity gets 10% too.

@munch I think the best bonus game is Spooky Bonus. As for which was most fun to develop, that's tricky because all of them were intense projects with fixed (seasonal) deadlines.

My first two bonus games (Xmas and Easter) were made in BlitzPlus and because the language wasn't OOP, I found it awkward to use. Also I was learning the ropes about how to make a proper commercial game. Both games flopped btw. I did have fun though and was naively positive about how well they would do. After they shipped I changed to BlitzMax and made my Grey Alien BlitzMax Framework and made The Wonderful Wizard of Oz (as a contractor), and then reused the engine to make Holiday Bonus.

I enjoyed making Holiday Bonus with iPete2 from these forums (he sorted out the graphics) and made a great intro movie from several 3D rendered stills. At the time casual games didn't really have intros so ours stood out. Also I got into the Christmas spirit when making the game and I knew it was way better than Xmas Bonus, my first game. I had high hopes it would do really well, but actually it didn't sell that well despite people liking it. However, that was just the first year, but it kept selling every year and in fact sold better in later years when I made the GOLD version and localised versions. So in the end it turned out great.

Spring Bonus started off fun, especially as I'd just quit my corporate job at Big Fish Games, and Spring was on the way. However, my wife got really ill towards the end of the project and finishing it on time became a nightmare. I still think it's a great game and I'm proud of it. It's an improvement over Holiday Bonus because it has level goals, and the golden eggs mechanic.

Spooky Bonus was supposed to be a quick project to bring in a bit of money because I was broke. But I got really into the design, and I worked with James Mintram (Lemon Moose Games) who helped with some of the new mechanics such as the shop/decoration screen, powerups, and the stone blocks. It was an intense project, and having to review and test someone else's code so close to the shipping deadline was a bit stressful. This game is quite a lot more advanced than Spring Bonus though with all the new features. Basically each of my match-3s isn't just a reskin, it has incremental (sometimes large) improvements.

As for Vancouver, it is a great city! In fact this weekend there's a Full Indie conference running there, which is a group I co-founded with a friend. Jeff Vogel is one of the speakers, which is pretty awesome. However, I moved back to the UK 3 years ago!
