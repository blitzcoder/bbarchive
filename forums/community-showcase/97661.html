GfK(Posted 2012) [#50]
OK, here's my review having downloaded and played Candy III.

First off, it gets downloaded with CNET's download manager, which tries to install toolbars and all sorts of crap if you aren't on your toes. Second, the installer dumped three shortcuts on my desktop and did not give me the option to NOT have it do that. So I'm annoyed instantly, from the off.

Second, the game defaults to French and remains so even if I run the config tool, set it to English and click 'Save', but, no comprendez. I still have to endure the thing in French. The config tool itself it awful. Tiny window, black screen, default Blitz3D font. No effort on your part at all.

Upon finally running the game, I quickly notice that any potential escape route has been deliberately cut off - there is NO Quit option. In a state of near-panic, I jabbed the Escape button, which started the game. I frenziedly attacked the Escape key again and was promptly returned to desktop. This is incredibly poor design.

So, the game then. I'm afraid it doesn't get much better. There are no instructions whatsoever - not even French ones. I hit the cursor keys at random and the dog set off at a rate of knots, until I'd moved about five yards forward when I was treated to a cut-scene (I assume) of a flying saucer coming down for no apparent reason. I was unable to move for several seconds. When I finally started moving I ran towards what appeared to be a group of penguins. Dogs chase birds, right? So it made sense to do that. Candy must be allergic to penguins because she had an epileptic seizure when I got close, before doing a gigantic backwards somersault right back to where I started.

I spotted that the penguins are around something in a cage - I presume that I'm supposed to liberate whatever is in the cages from the psychopathic penguins but I'm given no guidance or even any cryptic clue as to how I might go about doing so, and yet for some reason, every twenty seconds I am treated to a cut-scene of a sphere; presumably meant to be an asteroid, falling from the sky. During this visual feast, this.... eyegasm of blandness, I am unable to move the mouse, do anything or even press Escape to get the hell out of there and preserve what little sanity I have left.

I seem to die almost randomly... and after almost seven minutes now of attempting to make sense of this, coupled with the intolerable and un-turn-offable 'music', I was beginning to actually wish I WAS dead.

OK, I didn't go into this thinking I was going to be treated to an amazing game. In fact, I was kind of only going to look for the bad stuff from the outset. But the truth is - and I've written some crap games myself in my time - this is not a game. It's an ordeal. At no point did I recoil in surprise and go "oh yeah that's actually good". There is NOTHING good here. The music is bad, the graphics are awful and the animation, incredibly, is worse. The gameplay is.... well, WHAT gameplay? Everything that happens in the game; the psycho penguins, the pointless ufo/asteroid cut-scenes, the complete lack of instruction or direction, is just annoying.


So, the scores on the doors:

Graphics: 2/10 - Awful models, even worse animation.
Sound: 1/10 - Apart from a couple of high-pitched vocals and the God-awful music, there's nothing.
Gameplay: 1/10 - I jest ye not - there simply isn't any. I've given it 1/10 because there might be somebody out there who likes backward-somersaulting dogs.
Execution: 1/10 - It runs, but I wish it didn't. Terrible design and implementation throughout.
OVERALL: 1/10

Summary:
Candy to the Wheelie Bin.
