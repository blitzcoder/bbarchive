MGE(Posted 2008) [#10]
Thanks for the comments! :)

@GA: Fair points, all very true.

The games I'll release will probably never be a portal's cup of tea. :( I'm not big on brain games, I'm more of a twitch coder. So I like lots of variety, short quick diversions, etc, etc. At first when I started this game I kept deciding which road to take, make it more puzzle oriented or go with a dynamic arcade style similar to Dr.Mario, Columns, Tetris, etc, etc. In the end, my twitch roots won. :)

Besides, being a Holiday themed game I thought it would be better to go with something very simple, easy to play, with lots of diveristy to keep the players interested. With 20 backgrounds, 20 music tracks, several mini games, new pieces and events in later levels, I'm hoping there's enough content there to warrant some interest. ;)

Luckily, I was able to keep the overall initial development costs very low, so it doesn't even have to come anywhere close to "cleaning up" to make it worth while financially. :)

I'm happy with it, especially since it's my first "real" game. :)

Derron(Posted 2008) [#50]
Hmm... first grats for having it done ;D

Tested it some minutes on a non-widescreen screen.

While the presents in the bag on the left have a kind of global illumination-shadows, the presents in the playable field and the christmas tree don't have it.

Without shadows or slight darker borders the sprites miss really much contrast. I quit the game after some minutes because the background of the field and the sprites seemed to melt together. From my point of view you should really think about adding some soft shadow to the sprites or some kind of contrast-creating border. Same for the background: bring in some more contrast else it looks toooo soft.
The general style of the sprites (models) is really nifty the right grade of cartoon-ism I like.

The sound is - like GA already mentioned - a bit loud ;D. I don't know if a younger audience wants more noise coming from the speakers. Also think about adding some more SFX which can randomly replace others to avoid driving your customers ears crazy.
