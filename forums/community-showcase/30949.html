sswift(Posted 2004) [#14]
Puki:
you're too late, Michael already answered correctly. :-)

But you are correct, BOB stands for Blitter Object.


Sure, the PC doesn't have the hardware the Amiga had, but that doesn't matter. I needed a term other than "sprite" so that people won't confuse them with Blitz3D's billboard sprites. And I figured the Amiga fans would appreciate the homage. :-)



SwiftBOBS (Swift Blitter Object System) will be arriving shortly. It's nearly complete, and will be finished sometime today.

SwiftBOBS is a system for drawing 2D sprites, hereafter referred to as BOBS. You can create BOBS, move them around, and change their draw order all with standard Blitz-like commands. In addition, you can change their color, alpha, and blending mode. And, they support both masking and alpha maps.

Obviously blending comes as a speed hit, but the system has one optimized function for each type of blit to make it as fast as possible, and blitting a normal masked image with no special effects runs at the same speed as any normal image blitting command.

I'll be posting a full run down of all 22 commands after I finish the system and make a demo.
