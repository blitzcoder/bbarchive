Grey Alien(Posted 2007) [#1]
Hi everyone, I'm proud to present my latest game:

Fairway Solitaire!

It's a golf-themed solitaire game. Warning: Highly addictive!

I was contracted by Big Fish Games to make a downloadable version of their most popular online game community game of 2006. I worked very closely with a BFG game designer and an American artist - we made a great team and it was a lot of fun (and hard work)! It has taken 8.5 months to complete. That might seem a long time for a solitaire game, but check it out and you'll see why it took so long. The game has tons of features such as an avatar, storyline, shop, statistics, trophies, golf announcer + more ... and it's been SUPER polished.

I'm really proud of this game, it's my best one to date and has been made with my BlitzMax Game Framework which many people have purchased to speed up the development of their own games.

I hope that you enjoy it :-)

Grey Alien(Posted 2007) [#28]
well I'm not the artist, his name is Matt Laverty. He's very skilled, does all sorts including animation for Disney and Nickleodeon. Naturally as the programmer I have to lay all the art out and get changes made to stuff that's not right etc. It's an iterative process and some parts of the game went through multiple (sometimes tiresome) redesigns until we felt they were right.

Yeah I thought some people might have been thinking I was gonna pull a blinder, but the reality is that it still takes 8 months to make a solitaire game with enough polish to sell and that it probably takes even longer to make something "spectacular and original". The original twist to this game is the golf theme including the sand traps, water hazards and irons which change the solitaire gameplay in a way which has neve been done before. Also it's not the "usual" varation of solitaire, it's a different sort (there's tons on wikipedia).

You know we could have easily worked on this game for several months more plugging in more features that we had plan and making lots of little tweaks and polish - but at the end of the day you've just got to get on and release it as often these tweaks make little difference to the final conversion rate and can take AGES.

As for the other thing, say I have an anim that lasts 2000ms and I want it slow at the start and fast in the middle and slow at the end, I developed a function that I could pass in the current value (say 500) and the max value (2000) and it will convert the value from a linear value into a value from 0 to 1 but using sine to make it smoother (slower at start, then faster, then slower).
