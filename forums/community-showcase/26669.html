Anthony Flack(Posted 2003) [#4]
Mm, definitely strip out some polygons - forget about he spheres on the wheels, and all the ladder/light details on the body - better just to draw this stuff on. Likewise with the mudguards - no need to give them sides, it's wasted. And stuff like the exhaust pipe/axles have too many polygons around the circumference, too. The way it is now, it'll choke up too much graphics resources for no good reason.

That's just a practical/performance consideration if you actually want to use this truck in a game. You need to keep the number of polygons down as low as possible. The model itself looks good - but I really don't think that's the proper way to attach truck axles to the chassis!

jfk EO-11110(Posted 2003) [#7]
po, yes, always try to keep the Polycount low. Especially don't use Tris where they are not really required. It's a basic Law :) If you use lots of Tris, then please for something like round shapes of a Porsche or something :)

The reason is: the less Tris you Truck uses, the more Trucks can be in the Game. Trucks and other things, of course.

Have a look at Lithunwrap, Key posted a link in "other tools". Then try to draw the Ladder onto the Texture aso.

Hint: in Lithunwrap> Select all, UV-Map:Box-Mode, then horizonticly Flip the left side of the Truck and lay it over the right side... Feel free to ask when something isn't clear with Lithunrap (happens sometimes :) )
