Old-fashioned RPG games are my true passion when it comes to gaming, and therefore I'm proud to present an addition to this genre from *my* hand!

The Fairy Tale!

As this game is not completely finished yet, I cannot put anything of for download or even buying, but I've made a website with all known about the game, plus it contains a list of everything I still have to do yet...

My first RPG project was "Power Of The Rings", an RPG saga containing two episodes yet, in which history, present and future were mixed, and also set up for a mature audience since the way of language is not always suided for kids and it contained some mature jokes...

On "The Fairy Tale" I went into a diffrent road... I choose the chliché elements, as elves, dwarves, fairies and dragons, but I set them in a new world and made a few tiny adjustments to their original properties, and the world of Phantasar, where the story of the game takes place in was born... The story-line was at first very cliché, however picking up my hammer, my saw and my screwdriver I been fixing up the story-line and I must say, I'm really satisfied with what I have now...

Lots of friends of mine, plus myself have been putting a lot of effort in the game, trying to make it a very good game...

Basically, I didn't went for a revolutionary game... The object was to create a game I would like play myself, and as a true addict of this genre I have a vision of what is good and what not... I've also been playing a lot of other RPG games, because A. I love them and B. to make my sights bigger...

The Fairy Tale is a very old-school RPG game (but that old-style is still very popular among the true fans)...
I might go into the world of 3D for my next project...

As it will be hard to give up any ideas since you didn't play the game yet (I'll upload a demo version as soon as possible), you may still give some general impressions on the story and screenshots as put on the site in the link I gave you...

Some other things that came cross my mind, when I woke up today... (I always dream / think too much)

These are some ways to speed up your filesystem:

1.
Check your image file formats.
In the small version there were some files doubled?!?
Do not use these PCX, PLEASE!!!

For Animframes -> use Png
For static Pictures (no transparency) -> use Jpg

An example:
The "portret.bmp" 238 kb bmp, 46 kb as pcx, 22 kb as png

Hint: Use Acdsee (trial) or any gfx program to convert existing images to png

2.
You said that you must load a lot of things/data.
So why not get rid of some not really needed images.
You could draw the fractal screens and the battle border screens, instead of having images for them in v-mem!
That would reduce the overal imagenumber.

3.
Furthermore you could try to load all your images into systemmem (banks) and move them to v-mem if needed.
This method is quite a bit faster than loading from HDD each time. - Also try to reduce your filenumber by putting animations of the same king in one (bigger) image. The less (different) files, the faster the loading will be!

These are only some methods of speeding up loading!!!
