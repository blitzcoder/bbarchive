RemiD(Posted 2017) [#15]
smoothinggroups are used to define how a shape/partofashape will be structured, that is if triangles will share vertices (with average normals) or use duplicated vertices (with different normals) in order to produce the wanted lighting/shading.
This is especially important if you use vertices lighting/shading (with dx7 lights or vertex color)
for example, for the same shape, a cube, it could be made of 8vertices 12triangles (without duplicated vertices, which corresponds to a smoothinggroup of 180) or 24vertices 12triangles (with some duplicated vertices, which corresponds to a smoothinggroup of 89)

so, you want to use different smoothinggroups for different shapes/partsofshapes so the lighting/shading looks convincing.
an illustration of the impact of smoothinggroups on different shapes


the value corresponds to the maximum angle between 2 triangles
if the angle is smaller, the same vertex will be used by the 2 triangles (shared vertex)
if the angle is bigger, a different vertex will be used by the 2 triangles (duplicated vertex)
so basically with a smoothinggroup of 180, the shape will be welded (there will be no duplicated vertices)
and with a smoothinggroup of 89 (90-1) some parts of the shape will be welded, some not (there will be some duplicated vertices)

it seems that your scene only use a smoothinggroup of 180 for all shapes, hence the weird lighting/shading...
