taumel(Posted 2010) [#84]
This weekend i was forced lying in my bed and due to this had some time playing through Eschalon Book II. Version 1.04 felt a lot better than the initial version to me, which i thought was kind of rushed.

I enjoyed playing through it and with the experiences already gathered with the demo before, i quickly made my way in the beginning. What i liked is the overall silence, the space and the lonesome feeling of the game. Eschalon just doesn't scream at you like a lot of other RPGs.

Maybe it's because playing as a thief is a bad choice but with such a character i found it quite hard on the default settings. What i really dislike in Eschalon is that the difference if you buy and sell something is so extreme. In my opinion this could be better done and you should get more for your stuff when you're selling it. If there was one problem i had, then it was money, money, money, i mean a constant lack of it.

There was one situation where i knew how to do it but with the resources i had, it would take me ages, so i fired up an hex editor, gave myself enough to buy some potions, repair my armour and weapon (repairing magical stuff is too expensive) and voila, the game was fun again.

Eschalon in V1.04 feels a lot better than the first one but it still needs balancing here and there.

I liked the landscapes but at somepoint boom the game was simply over. I was quite surprised because dunno i would have expected something more to come. This was really a surprise as i somehow missed the point when it should have been obvious that i'm coming to an end, i would have expected about 1/3 or 1/4 more to come.

Anyway overall i've defiantely enjoyed it but it could do better on a few aspects. Maybe it's primary a niche product but for a broader audience i find the price too high.

And on a final note, i experienced zero crashes or hickups with 1.04, so very well done. Oh and i still find killing rats extremely boring and would prefer less stereotypical monsters instead.
