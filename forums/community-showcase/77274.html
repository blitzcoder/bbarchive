JustLuke(Posted 2008) [#50]
I don't think that it's fair or realistic to compare a mass-market console FPS to an indy PC game that has no marketing or funding, no professional production values, and little popularity. The only people who are likely to purchase and play an online version of Xylvan's game are people who (a) are aware of Xylvan's existing games and (b) enjoyed playing them.

Let's say that only 10 people bought the single player version of Lights of Dreams. Let's also say that out of these people, 80% of them enjoyed the game enough to be interested in playing an online version. That's only 8 potential players for the online game. An online community of 8 people who most likely won't be online at the same time as each other.

If Xylvan decided to really promote his game he might attract new potential players to the franchise. Since they're unlikely to buy an indy MMORPG sight unseen they'll probably want to evaluate it. So they download a demo, log on, and find the gameworld devoid of players - or, if they are extremely lucky, containing our aforementioned 8 existing players. Do you think that they'd be impressed by this? Or likely to buy the game? Or even continue playing the demo?

This is why I suggest that Xylvan might be better concentrating on developing his single player games.
