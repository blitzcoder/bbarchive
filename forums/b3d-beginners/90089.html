SLotman(Posted 2010) [#5]
No! You can't export anymore from MAX to .B3D, unless you're exporting to .3DS, and then you loose texture info, some UV mapping, etc.

The 'b3dpipeline' (which works great) only works up to MAX9 - newer versions won't work. So, you'll have to resort to other programs to export/convert stuff, making it a lot more problematic than it should be.

So, unless you aiming to buy Max9 or older - and specifically to export to .B3D, then forget it.


Naughty Alien(Posted 2010) [#6]
..informations regarding 3dsmax versions related to B3D pipeline, SLotman said, are correct, however, you can export entirely lightmapped mesh out of 3dsmax on other ways, without involving B3D as a export mesh..options may be use of UU3D or use of 2 identical meshes exported, one with difuse textures and second with lightmap only and then process both and copy color values from lightmapped one over difuse one in runtime (doable and works like a charm, tested it myself), and then once that process is done, simply free from memory lightmapped loaded mesh and you will have your level perfectly lightmapped with Vray, Mental ray or whatever suits your needs ..
