Shifty Geezer(Posted 2004) [#6]
Eko - The CreateMirror creates a flat plane floor to mirror your scene. The mirror image is created by rendering the whole scene from a different, reflected view . This doubles polygon count. To have mirrored object working this way would be EXTREMELY costly, every surface of every object needing a seperate rendering.

What you want just isn't done in computer games. Instead games use a workaround with sphere/cube mapped environment images that give the impression of a reflective surface, such as shown in the Teapot demo. By using the right textures you can give the impression of reflective chrome etc.
