Drak(Posted 2010) [#4]
I've been working on an RPG off an on for years. I got burnt out on it a long time ago, but began working on it again recently. One of the biggest obstacles to overcome is the sheer magnitude of media you will require.

No i'm not trying to persuade you to not make one, it's actually quite fun to work on them.

This is what I did:
What I would do first is make a very small rpg. I'm talking one map, a few characters, a few monsters, a few buildings, just a sampling of everthing. Once you can succesfully have your character interact with a small environment like this, add a second area or map for the player to go to. By the time you get a couple of areas done like this you'll have a great knowledge of the workings of a game like this, and be the rest is more or less adding new areas, npcs, monsters, etc.

But, like I said rpg's tend to have a huge collection of media.

Drak(Posted 2010) [#6]
The only program I purchased was T.ED terrain editor to make the terrains in. As far as placing objects like trees, houses, etc, I made my own editor for that.

I use Anim8or for modeling, even though not many people like it. I absolutely love it. It can export 3ds files.

Pacemaker is free now, which can import 3ds models, bone and animate them, and export in B3D. This is what I use for all my animating. It's a fabulous tool.
