I'm certainly familiar with how texture UV data works... but what effect does the W value have? I've come across this before in other applications, but never found any clear explanation. ...So what's it do???


sswift(Posted 2004) [#2]
W does nothing in Blitz. It's just listed for future expansion.

A texture as you know it is 2D. W is for 3D textures. 3D textures aren't used much yet except in 3D rendering packages.

Sometimes the W will be used to combine a 2D and a 1D texture to make a pseudo 3D texture, for lighting for example, but again that's not something you can do in Blitz.
