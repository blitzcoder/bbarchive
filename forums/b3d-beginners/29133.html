MSW(Posted 2004) [#4]
If image quality is important, then use a multi-pass approch to rendering...it can slow the frame rate but you would be able to see both near and far without problems...

1)set the camera range from say 5,000 to 20,000
2)renderworld
3)clear off only the Z-buffer
4)then set the camera range from 1 to 5,000
5)renderworld, again
6)then flip the buffer and repeat from step 1
