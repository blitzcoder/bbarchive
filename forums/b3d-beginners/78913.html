IcyDefiance(Posted 2008) [#4]
I'd recommend Blender instead of Maya. It has a bit of a learning curve, because of a strange UI, but once you get used to it, you'll wish everything was made like that. I know I do. It is almost as good as Maya, but it's a bit of a power hog, and shouldn't be used on weak computers. And it's free!

Best of all, it can export into a ton of formats: 3D Studio, AC3D, COLLADA, FBX Export, DXF, Wavefront OBJ, DEC Object File Format, DirectX, Lightwave, MD2, Motion Capture, Nendo, OpenFlight, PLY, Pro Engineer, Radiosity, Raw Triangle, Softimage, STL, TrueSpace, VideoScape, VRML, VRML97, X3D Extensible 3D, xfig export.

I think it can also import most, if not all of these, but I haven't gone through and checked.
