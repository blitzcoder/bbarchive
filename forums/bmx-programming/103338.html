Kryzon(Posted 2014) [#2]
On fullscreen mode, and at least on Windows, when the mouse pointer is at the edge of the screen and you still move the mouse in that direction the operating system does not register a "mouse move" event, so to your application it will look like the mouse is stationary.

What some games do, like Warcraft 3 for example, is to detect when the mouse is at the edge of the screen -- 8 possible directions, including diagonally -- and scroll the camera by a fixed amount. In Warcraft 3 you can edit this amount as the "Mouse scroll speed" setting.
If you click and hold the mouse and then move the pointer outside of the screen, the camera starts moving and your selection area increases with this, so you can select units that are in an arrangement bigger than the viewport size by using this feature.

The scrolling stops when you move the pointer away from the edge of the screen.
