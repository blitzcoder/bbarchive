H&K(Posted 2011) [#7]
Names, title or phrases are not themselves subject to copyright in their own right.
Such items may easily be duplicated by coincidence, and are therefore not considered unique or substantial enough to be awarded copyright protection in their own right.
Copyright laws are actually very restrictive, and to apply these laws to items that may be duplicated coincidentally, or that may be legitimately used in unrelated instances, (such as a name, title or phrase), makes no sense.
Generally it is assumed that all parties with a similar name or product name wish to protect the integrity of that name, but do not in law own that name.
The only times the Law is considered to have any involvement is when one product/company is trying to pass-itself off as the another one. So when it comes down to it, unless dodge-it is a name of another game I would still consider using it.

[Im not a lawyer, but was given business legal advice a couple of years ago]

Names are trademarked and not copyrighted.

From Legalzoom

"Copyright does not cover intellectual property such as titles, names, short phrases, and slogans; familiar symbols or designs; or mere variations of typographic ornamentation"

"A trademark protects a word, phrase, symbol or design (or a combination of these), that identifies and distinguishes the goods or services of one person or company from those of others"
