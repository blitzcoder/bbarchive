sswift(Posted 2003) [#46]
It doesn't make textures look horribly over exposed. It brightens them when the color in the lightmap is greater than 127, and darkens them when it is less than 127. 2x brightens them up to 2x their orginal brightness.

Without 2x blending lightmaps can only make textures darker than they are by default. This does not look very realistic, and scenes often appear dull.

No One Lives Forever - 2x Multiply
http://www.gamescreenshots.com/fullpic.asp?category=pc&pic_id=35&game_id=1&gallery=0&picOrder=0

Quake 2 - 1x Multiply
http://www.gamescreenshots.com/fullpic.asp?category=pc&pic_id=36693&game_id=415&gallery=0&picOrder=0

This is a better comparison though. This is a dark area from Serious Sam. Note how the lighting is much more vibrant. That's 2x in action.
http://www.gamescreenshots.com/fullpic.asp?category=pc&pic_id=67461&game_id=21&gallery=0&picOrder=0
