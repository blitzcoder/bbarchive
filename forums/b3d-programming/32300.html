Me again! The Egyptian stuff (if it was my level with the rising door, flaming torches and dripping water), was lit using CShop.

This is then used as a level for Singularity Engine to show - which uses .csm not b3d or .x .

The reason was that the blending and lighting which could be achieved via Josh's .csm method was - at that time - better due to other blending modes he developed/used.

That is no longer the case now as B3d has the x2 mode and I guess as a result, Josh has re-instigated the .b3d export, along with .x and half life .map format.

There could be a bone of contention here because I light stuff from CShop in CShop.

Everything else I lightmap in Gile[s].

I tried last night to light something done in CShop - in Giles - and it didn't work too well. In fact I have decided to go to Max for that project, and it will be lit in Gile[s].

The latest version of Gile[s]also fixed a curious problem which meant that some items which were rotated in Max, came in rotated incorrectly.

This was stopping me use it for a lot of my stuff. Although that problem has gone, another may still exist with the positioning of those rotated objects, I have still to check this out, so don't quote me or base a decision on this.
