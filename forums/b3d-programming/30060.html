Gabriel(Posted 2004) [#2]
I don't want to get too specific, because that's really down to personal preference. 50 people will say Intel, 50 will say AMD. 50 will say ATI, 50 will say Nvidia.

So generally, if you want your machine to be fairly usable in 2-3 years, you're really looking at top end hardware now. CPU's are reaching something of a plateau, so you need not get the very fastest there, but videocards are still moving along fast, so you'll want the best you can afford. I would also recommend getting as much ram as you can afford, because it's the most cost-effective upgrade you can buy.

I don't recommend buying any prebuilt PC. I recommend finding a local shop or an online store that will let you design a PC to your own specifications and build it for you. ( That's assuming you're not confident enough to buy the components and build it yourself. )
