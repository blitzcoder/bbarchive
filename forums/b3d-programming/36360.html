Bot Builder(Posted 2004) [#2]
All I know is a pretty lowcost workflow (note I haven't made a game yet :P):

Wings3D - Object modeling (free - http://www.wings3d.com/)

Ultimate Unwrap - unwrapping models (unwrapping is basically telling where parts of a texture go) this also functions VERY well as a converter betweeen 63 model formats ($40 http://www.unwrap3d.com/).

Gimp - Texturing (free - http://www.gimp.org/)

Lightmap - Gile[s] for quality + support (developer is a blitz user) http://www.frecle.net/giles/home.htm - €40. Slim shady for being free :P http://www.melog.ch/slimshady/

Terrain - take your pick http://www.blitzbasic.com/toolbox/toolbox.php?cat=7

Level editers - cartography shop (http://www.leadwerks.com/) , quill (http://shop.idigicon.com/pdetails.asp?id=418), or maplet (free from accounts section)

Check out the toolbox http://www.blitzbasic.com/toolbox/toolbox.php

Bot Builder(Posted 2004) [#4]
ah. well, it all depends on what you're making. For instance:

Outdoor adventure game level:

*Create terrain in Total Terrain (free :D)
*Create trees in treeD (requires giles to convert to b3d)
*Create static meshs in Wings3d
*Unwrap meshs with UU or lithunwrap (free)
*Texture with gimp or photoshop
*All the while positioning meshs and props in TerraED

(obviously tools can be interchanged)


jfk EO-11110(Posted 2004) [#5]
my fps stuff:

-map design with maplet
-adding textures to the map with decorator
-create furniture and stuff in milkshape or any modeler and uvmap it in lithunwrap, edit textures in Gimp or Photoshop
-adding furniture and lightmapping to map in slimshady
-using my inhouse placement tool to add pickable game items, doors etc.
-edit and animate characters (CharacterFX, Pacemaker, 3dsMAX)
-use inhouse tool to record waypoints for enemies etc
-Use Modplug to make some music
-Use Goldwave to edit sounds
-Use ogg Vorbis encoder to compress WAV to OGG
-use Molebox to pack the whole thing
-upload
