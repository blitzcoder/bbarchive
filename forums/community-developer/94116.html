Importing 3ds often results in "exploded" children, they are moved away from their position somwhow. Ths is usually caused by some pivot offset imcompatiblities in the way 3ds max works.

You may however export them with children in CharacterFX, at least I remember so. If they load correctly, they should also be exported correctly. I successfully imported multi-children 3ds and animated some of the children in a rotate/move/scale way (vs. the bones animation normally used in CharacterFX). Well, it's a couple of years ago, so I can't give any guaranties.
