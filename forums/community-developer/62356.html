jhocking(Posted 2006) [#4]
It's at the top of your screen, just below the Home, Products, etc tabs. That's where you can get b3d exporters for several tools, including 3D Studio Max.

"Best" is a relative term. 3D Studio Max is certainly one of the best tools around for doing pro work, and if you intend to advance your skills later then you will want that, but for simple stuff to begin with you might consider a lesser tool to fit your budget. It doesn't really matter which one you choose, despite all the righteous anger people pour into these debates. Just make sure it can export animation to b3d file format (or to .x so that you can convert using Ultimate Unwrap.)
