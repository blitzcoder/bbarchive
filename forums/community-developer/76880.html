Gabriel(Posted 2008) [#2]
Depends what you want it for. For pure poly modelling, it's one of the very best. It's poly modelling tools beat out most of the high end tools. It reminds me of Wings, which I know is used by a number of artists who own 3dsMax, Maya, Lightwave, etc, simply because the workflow for poly modelling is so good. I don't think there's anything else at this price point which remotely compares with it, apart from Wings itself, which is still lacking in some areas compared to Silo.

For detailing and sculpting, it's a poor man's ZBrush, with the emphasis on poor as the last version I tried was still very buggy. If you really want ZBrush but you can't afford it, I'd say either keep saving or manage without it, because Silo isn't going to compare.
