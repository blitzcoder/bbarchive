jfk EO-11110(Posted 2006) [#3]
I am using MAGIX video deluxe. In the UK it's named "MAGIX movie editor" or something like that. Some companies sell older versions (eg. version of 2005) for little money. It's very beginner friendly. You can drag'n drop etc. movies and audio on the tracks easily. It also includes excellent noise reduction tools. It's not far away from the power of adobe premiere, but much easier to learn, and lots cheaper.
