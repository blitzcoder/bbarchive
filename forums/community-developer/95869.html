Kryzon(Posted 2011) [#3]
I have never seen such a feature in it.

The two ways to reproduce this (albeit manually) are:
- Copy and paste the first melody and move it to where the second melody should start. Go through all these copied notes, changing their durations and tones so they actually play the second melody (while keeping the velocities from the original). This way is faster.

- When you double click any note on the Piano Roll a property box appears, where you can set with a knob the note's velocity (indirectly knowing its current velocity too). So go through every note in the first melody, open properties, see what's the velocity, then go through every note in the second melody and knob their velocities in to follow the same ordered values. This way is slower and you'll spend a lot of double-clicks.
