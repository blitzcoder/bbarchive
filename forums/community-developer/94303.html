The main advantage is you tick a few angles, how many frames you want from the anim sequence (ie. every 10 frames) and render it.
. It then spits out sprites (see the sample output on the download pages)

http://www.d-grafix.com/view.php?img=sf-step1
http://www.d-grafix.com/view.php?img=sf-step2
http://www.d-grafix.com/view.php?img=sf-step3
http://www.d-grafix.com/view.php?img=sf-step4

It also allows you to save the config for a particular render (useful if you are making say.. an rpg with loads of characters that have the same rendering rules)

I use fragmotion too but i wrote SF as a deliberate acompanyent to fragmotion/MShape originally as the b3ds it converts and makes work well with b3d (which is what the renderer is written in). It also works with blender output.
