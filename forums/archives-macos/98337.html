It looks like a kernel panic in the NVIDIA graphics driver. I would try first installing OS X on top of itself in the hope that overwriting the driver will solve it. If that fails you could look for a 3rd party replacement but they're hard to find and unreliable so I would instead make an appointment at an apple store and show them the logs, if replacing the driver doesn't fix it it's likely to be a hardware problem with the graphics card.

If you're really desperate to get off apple hardware you could build a hackintosh, but you do run the risk of being blocked from development so probably not worth it.

That is *shockingly* bad luck with mac hardware. I have only ever had 2 macs with any hardware failures in 20+ years (first gen iMac which went wrong 3 times but they eventually pressed boards that worked, and a powerbook that had it's screen just up an die for no reason). I do know someone who had 2 chronic problem macs back to back, but by and large it's pretty rare to have anything more than the occasional issue as a result of a manufacturing flaw (no manufacturing or QC process if flawless). If you're still under applecare and you've had that many hardware problems you could ask to get the system swapped, they did it for a friend of mine after his Powerbook Tianium lost a keyboard and then the monitor within 2 weeks of each other. It was cheaper for them to replace the system than to risk having to keep fixing it (since it was the motherboard cooking the other components) so they just replaced it.
