Cheese(Posted 2011) [#4]
I truly agree.

Throughout all of my life, cross compatibility has always been a limiting priority on my list. I've also always believed that game design, recreational or professional, shouldn't have to be about redundantly rewriting engines or low level code in order to maintain the aspect of cross compatibility. Mojo has not only successfully taken this weight off of my shoulders, but I'm now floating with freedom unprecedented. Never in my life did I think that a BRL framework, let alone any product, would patch this issue for me.

In short: Monkey is now one of my favorite languages, being rock solid in a very structural manner, but also strangely familiar in syntax. Mojo makes me weightless, as it truly is what it's name implies.
For your hard work and dedication, I recognize you, Mark Sibly, along with the others from the community who've gone out of their way to help you improve the language and framework thus far. I wish you great fortune for making my thought process, along with the development process of many others, much less bleak and much more exciting.

Thank you,
- Brandon.
