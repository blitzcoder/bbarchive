muddy_shoes(Posted 2012) [#9]
Xaron, that really isn't how trademarks work. Hasbro do own the trademark on the word Battleship as it applies to computer games, in the US at least. Here, look: http://tess2.uspto.gov/bin/showfield?f=doc&state=4006:53jjn2.2.16

Edit: And also in Europe. Application: 006894984 if you care to go and look.

You can trademark Battleship just the same as you can trademark Windows or Apple or any other "generic" word. You seem to be confusing the concept of trademark with copyright. Hasbro cannot use a gameplay copyright to stop you publishing a game that plays like Battleship, that's true, but they can do something about you using their trademark.

The match does not have to be exact for someone to successfully claim an infringement. The general principle is whether the name can be seen as passing off as the trademark. The further away from the exact word the less likely any claim would be successful or made at all, of course.

If Hasbro's lawyers notice your game and feel the need to act it seems most likely that you'll never see the inside of a courtroom to argue the case. The app stores will probably decide if there's a legitimate claim first and foremost. Do you think Apple will feel like standing between you and Hasbro to fight for the idea that a space strongly differentiates your title from Hasbro's trademark?
