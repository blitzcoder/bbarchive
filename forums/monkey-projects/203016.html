xlsior(Posted 2012) [#18]
Reminds me a bit of the ancient 'tapper' game: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Root_Beer_Tapper

Although one big difference (which made tapper -much- harder) is that in tapper you die if you throw something down a lane without there being anything to hit. (glass flies of the bar, smashing it, lose a life)

Some constructive critisism:

In the current setup, you game allows you to pretty much continually go up and down the lanes, shooting twice in each lane without looking, rinse & repeat. There is no real penalty that I could see for wasting ammo...
shoot-shoot-up-shoot-shoot-up-shoot-shoot-up-shoot-shoot-down-shoot-shoot-down, etc. If the zombies are even remotely equally distributed among the floors, you'll be able to beat pretty much every level that way.

It would become a lot harder if you wouldn't only enforce a number of bullets per lane, but also a total number of bullets on the screen at a time -- that way you can't just mindlessly shoot two bullets down an (almost) empty floor because doing that may not leave you with any bullets for the zombies near the end on floor above it.
