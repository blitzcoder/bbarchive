xlsior(Posted 2008) [#4]
It's a limitation present in most keyboards, and there is no fix, other than buying a keyboard without it... but even if you do, the vast majority of your customers will have the same problem.

It's an issue that goes back decades, and is due to technical limitations in the construction of the earlier keyboard standards, which persist to this day.

Those lock-outs are one of the main reasons why so many games use the control, alt and shift modifiers as a 'fire'-button or item selection and such -- those keys don't interfere with any other key, and are always safe to use.
