Save as PNG instead, it uses lossless compression, unlike JPG which will smash your picture even on 'best quality' settings.


FlameDuck(Posted 2005) [#3]
Yes. JFIF is only suitable for photographs (hence the name Joint Photographic Expert Group) where the artefacting isn't generally noticable.

Depending on what application you use to make your images, you may have settings to do with how the PNG is saved. You'd want to set it to compress as much as possible if you have that option. Some software gives you a choice of what kinds of compression it does, while others just assume some preset.
