Galdy... most intend with "tab", others intend with "space" - the most and best practice is to use tab AND on export (eg. if working with others - who use different editors) convert the tabs to a fixed amount of spaces (most: 4 spaces = 1 tab).
Why? a Tab can be XX spaces wide when converted to fixed-width-chars.

Advantage of tabs: 2 tabs is in all cases one "depth" more than 1 tab... does not matter if 1 tab equals to 4 spaces or 6.

ziggy(Posted 2012) [#5]
BLIde should auto-indent as you type, using its autocompletion system. Also it inherits indentatio from previous line.
That said, if what you want is format the identation of a previously written source code document, you can do so on "Edit / Advanced / Format whole document" This option will scan and format the whole document, including expressions, tabs, spacing, datatype shortcuts, etc etc.

You can also select a whole block of code and press TAB to ident it, or Shift+Tab to decrease identation.
