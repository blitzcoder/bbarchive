Derron(Posted 2016) [#7]
Under the assumption that you want what I described:
you need to be able to rotate a vertex (A) around two other vertices (B an C).

AB form a line, BC too - same for AC. So you could use the mathematic formulas you learned for triangles (and quads - all 4 angles = 360° etc.).

When now rotating "A" along BC you know that the distance between A and BC is constant, only the angle changes.
