Yasha(Posted 2015) [#2]
Yes.

BlitzMax's form of OOP is quite basic and old-fashioned, but it will teach you the most important concepts. Polymorphism is present in BlitzMax, which is the heart of all OOP styles across all languages.

You don't need to use it though, in BlitzMax or in any other language. OOP is not the be-all-and-end-all in programming (although it seemed that way for a bit in the 90s).
