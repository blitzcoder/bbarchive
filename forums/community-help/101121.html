xlsior(Posted 2013) [#4]
Windows 7 puts its own 100MB boot partition at the beginning of the drive. It goes there first before it goes to XP on your main partition.

If you really need to get rid of the Windows 7 boot manager, you may need to get rid of the 100Mb partition, and then use the XP CD to repair your XP boot sequence
