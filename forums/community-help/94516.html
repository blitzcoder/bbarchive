xlsior(Posted 2011) [#3]
FYI: online automatic translations to Dutch are generally attrocious, and this is no exception. :-?

DUTCH:

Play - either 'Spelen' or 'Speel', both are used in this context.

How to Play - 'Hoe te spelen', although that does sound a tad awkward to me. You may want to consider an alternative, like 'Spel Handleiding' (game manual), 'Instructies' (instructions) or 'Uitleg' (explanation) (your automatic translation was missing a verb, and just said "how you the game".

Credits - Credits (dankbetuigingen = "Expression of thanks", very formal and not a word anyone would use)

More Games - Meer Spellen
