Do you really need AVI/DivX? It's a pretty outdated, inefficient codec -- depending on your needs, you may want to consider MP4/h.264 , takes up about 50% of the size of divx at the same visual quality.

Anyway, IIRC Avidemux can convert FLV to either AVI or MP4. Actual available formats may depend on what codecs you have installed on your PC.

Handbrake is also free, and great to convert to MP4 specifically.

ShadowTurtle(Posted 2015) [#7]
alternatively you can use ffmpeg directly (it is not a software with gui)

1. open url http://ffmpeg.zeranoe.com/builds/
2. scroll down
3. download a static variant
4. extract the file
5. copy the video in the extracted "bin" directory
6. open command-line tool (use google for more informations)
7. enter "dir path\to\directory"
8. enter "ffmpeg -i input.flv output.avi"
9. open url for more informations about all parameters https://www.ffmpeg.org/ffmpeg.html#Generic-options
