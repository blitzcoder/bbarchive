GfK(Posted 2011) [#3]
My 450D has a 1.6x crop factor so that gives me an effective 800mm to play with.

I couldn't really fault my Canon 55-250mm lens but when I'm doing a lot of my shooting at nature reserves and RAF bases, it is physically impossible to get as close as I'd like to. I'm also finding that the Sigma OS is far superior to the Canon IS, even when shooting at 500mm its pretty easy to get sharp handheld shots. Plus it has two OS modes - full OS, and horizontal only, which is perfect for taking pics of planes taking off.

By the way, I got my lens from http://www.procamerashop.co.uk/ - didn't post the link before as I hadn't used them before and wanted to see how good they were first. I ordered my lens on Sunday afternoon and it was here Tuesday morning before 9am, and it was over £200 cheaper than anywhere else. As far as I'm aware all of their stuff is imported, hence the price so you might have to be careful on warranties with some things. Definitely recommend them though.
