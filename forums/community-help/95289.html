Warner(Posted 2011) [#20]
About why I'm asked to do this: I don't understand why myself, but I'm not going to question that. It's also a job.
Normally, I teach programming, and I like history. I've been developing since 1984, so I've seen my share of the early platforms.
Throughout time, I have played games, but mostly to see how they were made.

I was thinking about splitting the topic into four parts, since I'll give four colleges/lessons:
-physiological history: how did platforms evolve
-sociological history: changes in who create/who play throughout history
-conceptual history: how did concepts change over time
-commercial history: how did the market change

Most books about gaming history seem to end at say 2003 with Wow, and speculate about how gaming will become immersive. But I think over the last +/- 10 years, many interesting developments have taken place, such as iPhone, Wii, N3DS, internet games.

Personally, I find Silas Warner, the org. author of Wolfenstein, an interesting part of gaming history. I think he was really influental, but is a bit underappreciated.
