Depending on what you need it for, If you are just after a personal server (<5 users) doing light stuff like perhaps file/printer share, svn/git/merc, email and a low traffic webserver (ie. a dev server) then you are in overkill mode there... That -vs- a 'normal' PC running win/linux. (or even an embedded or nas unit running linux which could do the same job. I use a netgear nas duo for these things. It cost 250 quid and has low power consumption and I use it for all of the above).
The power drain alone would be the first off putting thing of having a medium duty server like that....
