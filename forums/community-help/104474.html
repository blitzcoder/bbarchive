xlsior(Posted 2015) [#5]
there's a number of video capture programs out there -- but for best results, you may want to look at an image capture device such as these:
http://www.hauppauge.com/site/products/prods.html
e.g. PVR2 Gaming Edition

One advantage is that they off-load the video compression to a hardware device, so capturing the video itself doesn't bog down the computer while you're playing the game... Plus they allow you to mix in microphone audio real-time.

Plus, since they capture whatever the video out of your computer throws at it, it will work with all graphics modes: openGL, DirectX, windows, including xbox one, playstation 4, etc.
