xlsior(Posted 2011) [#2]
the vast majority of HDMI splitters out there take multiple inputs and a single output, to expand the number of HDMI ports available to a single TV.

For multiple *output* devices, do a search for "HDMI Matrix"

They are more expensive than simple multiple-to-single splitters, but multiple outputs is more complicated to accomplish thanks to how HDCP (content protection) works.

Yan(Posted 2011) [#4]
A 4x2 (4 inputs to 2 outputs) matrix is really overkill for what you want.

If you're not bothered about future expandability, and you want to save a few quid, then your situation only calls for a two way splitter.

A quick terminology guide to spare confusion:-
========= HDMI Gubbins =========
Switch   = Multiple to Single
Matrix   = Multiple to Multiple (*usually* in any combination)
Splitter = Single to Multiple

Switch Splitter = A switch with a nWay splitter on the output

As it appears that you've already purchased all you need, you should be good to go. :o)
