It really depends on the equipment and monitor settings too:

My LCD itself is set to full brightness, half contrast. (There's an annoying whistle tone from the backlight if brightness is below 90%)

In the ATI control panel I have both brightness and contrast set to their 50% mark, but do adjust the gamma settings: instead of he default 1.0, I'm using 0.55 to get significantly darker colors than typical.
The default settings give me eyestrain since the whites are too bright to my eyes.

At least ATI lets you set up multiple color profiles, so if I'm watching a video or something that's to dark I can hit hit some keyboard shortcuts to cycle between my default settings, and two brighter variations.
