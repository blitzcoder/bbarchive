Jesse(Posted 2011) [#8]
it can be the output audio jack on your laptop or the drivers for your motherboard or defective speaker system or the earphone jack is switched off.

check the speaker setting in your laptop. make sure that they are on and/or to an audible volume.
--
you can try testing your speakers with an mp3 player or any device that output sound that you can connect it too.
--
check the "control panel-> device manager " and see if it display any problems with the sound card. A yellow question mark might mean that it needs the appropriate sound drivers installed. check with your laptop manufacturer for the most recent sound drivers for windows 7. if they don't have it, I doubt drivesmith will be able to help you.
--
if the audio jack is bad your other alternative is to get usb speakers.
