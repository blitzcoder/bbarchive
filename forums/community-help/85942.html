Kryzon(Posted 2009) [#5]
http://www.noctua-graphics.de/english/freetex_e.htm

I also second what Vorderman said about cgtextures.com, it's really great.

But most textures there aren't seamless, so you need a bit of Photoshop skill, using the Offset filter and the Clone Stamp with a soft Brush (solves most cases of a homogenous texture like grass or rock). For stuff like bricks you need to use some special cropping.

http://www.myjanee.com/tuts/tilings/tilings.htm <- tutorials on Tiling with Photoshop.

Your pipeline in the end would be simply: Download a good, plain and homogenous texture for the material you want, edit in photoshop (tiling, color management), save in power of 2 format. It's much more satisfying to work out your own material this way.
