rIKmAN(Posted 2013) [#20]
Football players own the rights to their own name and image (or their club does), and thus can sell them to be used on shirts, cups, mugs, and.... games.

Why do you think Real Madrid spend £80m on players like Ronaldo and Bale?
Because they will help them win things (prize money) and they will make millions back by selling shirts with his name on, things with his face on etc that no other club can do because they don't own the rights.

It isn't a problem if you game is played by 100 people, but if it gets any noteworthy success then you will be looking at a cease and desist or a lawsuit.

If it was that easy to just use them then EA and Sports Interactive wouldn't have to pay licencing money to have real clubs, emblems, leagues and players in their games.
