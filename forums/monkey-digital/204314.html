Soap(Posted 2013) [#6]
If you don't need it you really should disable it in the browsers you use. There is no "safe place" when a 0-day like this happens. Say an admin of a popular site you go to is compromised - the site they admin is changed to serve up the exploit. Boom. You're owned too. And even with working AV exploits can distribute malware which is ahead of the curve of detection.

Uninstalling Java itself from your system isn't as necessary. Assuming you have it disabled in your browser and you're not running everything you come across online you will probably be safe.
